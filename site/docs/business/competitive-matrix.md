# Competitive Matrix

AI Delivery OS competes across three overlapping categories. The key insight: **do not compete on "best assistant."** Compete on systemic reliability — evidence quality, constrained execution, auditable decisions, and measurable improvements in delivery outcomes.

## Two Pricing Models

### Model A: Add-On Governance Tier *(Recommended for initial adoption)*

Price as an **add-on to existing copilots** — because most enterprise customers already have one. Target range: **$10–25/developer/month** with enterprise uplifts for compliance and support.

Anchor rationale: sits below or near the $19 baseline most orgs already pay, making "copilot + governance layer" psychologically acceptable as a combined spend.

### Model B: Platform License + Usage

Smaller per-seat platform fee plus **usage-based pricing** for high-cost agentic flows (Lane B/C). Matches broader market movement toward credits/usage in agentic platforms.

## What the Market Cannot Provide

The following capabilities are **not present in any mainstream incumbent** at the time of writing:

- **Structured, reliability-scored evidence** from the codebase before any agent plans work
- **Hard execution constraints** (forced_lane_minimum, TEE boundaries) that agents cannot override without logged human justification
- **Unified decision trace** connecting requirement through every stage to completion with replay capability
- **Causal cluster escalation** — severity-weighted warning accumulation that prevents alarm fatigue
- **Ground truth calibration loop** — 5% sampling, monthly replay, penalty weight tuning

These are the defensible moat. They compound over time as the decision ledger accumulates operational data specific to each customer's codebase.
